## [General Tree Evaluation for AlphaZero](https://github.com/albinjal/GeneralAlphaZero)

_Master's Thesis Project_

![Novel MVC Agent](/images/projects/general-alphazero.gif)

For my Master's thesis, I dug into the AlphaZero algorithm and found that the way it evaluates positions in the game tree could be improved. I "decoupled" the tree construction from the evaluation, which allowed me to experiment with new ways of estimating value.

## [Inference Compilation and Universal Probabilistic Programming](https://github.com/felixk29/compile_inference)

_Course Project: Seminar Probabilistic Programming_

This project was a deep dive into **Probabilistic Programming**. We worked on reproducing _Inference Compilation_, a technique where you compile a probabilistic program into a neural network artifact. This "artifact" then acts as a super-smart proposal distribution for importance sampling, making inference incredibly fast.

We implemented the LSTM-based proposal networks in **Gen** (a Julia library). It successfully learned to perform inference on a classic Burglar/Alarm Bayesian network. We also proposed a meta-learning extension to automatically tune the observation embeddings, which is usually a manual and tedious process.

## [Deep Hedging](https://github.com/appie-mathematics/Deep-Hedging)

_Course Project: Deep Reinforcement Learning_

![Training Process](/images/projects/deep-hedging.gif)

This was a fun reproduction project where we tackled the paper _Deep Hedging_. The premise is using Deep Reinforcement Learning to find optimal hedging strategies for a portfolio of derivatives, even when facing transaction costs.

We implemented the agents from scratch in PyTorch and set them loose on simulated markets. It was cool to see the agents actually learn to hedge effectively. You can see in the training GIF above how the agent (the red line) starts to figure out how to offset the risk of the portfolio (the blue line) over time, finding a strategy that minimizes the overall variance.

## [Bayesian Loss for Car Counting](https://github.com/albinjal/Bayesian-Car-Counting)

_Course Project: Seminar Computer Vision by Deep_

![Density Estimation Results](/images/projects/car-counting-bayesian-plus.png)

Counting dense crowds or traffic from overhead images is tough. For this project, we implemented **Bayesian Loss**, a method that uses point annotations to create a density contribution probability model. Essentially, instead of trying to predict exact points, the model predicts the _density_ of cars in an area.

We trained a VGG-19 network on the COWC dataset and the results were surprisingly good. We achieved a **71% reduction in Mean Squared Error** compared to the baseline.

![Comparison: Bayes+ vs Ground Truth](/images/projects/car-counting-comparison.png)

The coolest part was seeing it generalize. We took the model trained on standard dataset images and ran it over aerial shots of our own **TU Delft campus** from Google Earth.

![Generalization on TU Delft Campus](/images/projects/Google_earth_2.png)

It worked right out of the box! It correctly identified parking lots and ignored empty roads, showing it had learned a robust representation of what "dense traffic" looks like from above.

## [Taming Transformers](https://github.com/albinjal/CS4240-Reproduction-Project)

_Course Project: Deep Learning_

![Generated Samples](/images/projects/taming-transformers.png)

Transformers are taking over everything, including Computer Vision. In this project, we reproduced the **VQGAN + Transformer** paper. The idea is to use a VQGAN to compress images into discrete codebook indices, which a Transformer can then predict—allowing for high-resolution image synthesis.

We decided to port the entire VQGAN architecture to pure PyTorch (the original was a mix of Lightning and custom code). To verify it really worked, we trained it on **Montezuma's Revenge**, a distinctive Atari environment.

![Atari Reconstruction](/images/projects/taming-transformers-atari.png)

The reconstruction results (above) show the model successfully learning the complex, pixelated game states.
